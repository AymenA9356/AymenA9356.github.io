SEP10 Freedom Project

Context

The Freedom Project (FP) is a course-long project where we Apply the content we are learning in daily lessons to our own creation, Learn new content on our own, Reflect upon our learning in a blog, and Present our product and process.

Content

With just three days remaining until the expo, I struggled to come up with a one-minute hook for my presentation. Despite my efforts to condense it, my pitch was still over one minute long. The day before the expo, I panicked and attempted to remove some content. Eventually, I settled on an elevator pitch that highlighted my interest in aviation, my website showcasing past and current innovations, and my own innovative solution to enhance security in aviation. On the day of the expo, I was incredibly nervous and stressed about potentially messing up. When my turn came, I delivered my pitch, and the judges seemed either very interested or confused, as they asked numerous questions afterward. Seeking validation, I asked my friend how I did, and he reassured me that I did well. Despite having presented, I remained anxious as I awaited the results and wondered if I would be chosen to present at the showcase. Presenting in front of my classmates, who I knew personally, heightened my nervousness. However, I believed I did a good job with my slides. Before the class ended, Mr. Mueller announced the winner of the expo, and to my surprise, it was me. This meant I would have to present in front of a larger audience consisting of all SEP classes, family members, and Deloitte members, totaling around 170-180 people. The following day, I received feedback, which surprised me because I thought I had performed well. It turned out that I lacked a proper hook and failed to maintain good eye contact with the entire class. Additionally, I did not show my website effectively, although I did provide a yellkey link for anyone that was interested. The code snippets I presented were difficult to see as well. However, I did learn valuable skills throughout this experience, such as having a growth mindset and fostering creativity. I demonstrated patience and bravery by seeking help when faced with challenges. The judges recognized my innovation as being highly creative. Overall, despite the obstacles and areas for improvement in my presentation, winning the expo was a significant achievement. I can use the feedback received to enhance my presentation skills and continue developing my innovation. My innovation has a lot of positives. For example, on a global scale, it will have big systems all around the world because it will be in almost every airport. On a social scale, it will change individuals and the culture of Aviation security by almost eliminating all lines in security. On the economic side, it will increase the number of jobs and will also change the infrastructure.
